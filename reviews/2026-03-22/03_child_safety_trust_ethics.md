# Role 3: Child Safety / Trust Ethics Reviewer

Using the judge and competitor notes as the starting point.

## 7 trust/safety risks
1. A parent could overread an uncertain incident if the UI makes every alert look equally urgent.
2. The product could drift toward creepiness if guarded reveal is missing or too easy.
3. Remote pause actions can feel punitive if not explicitly tied to higher-confidence incidents.
4. Confidence is shown, but not yet used enough to limit action recommendations.
5. The child is still described mostly as a subject of monitoring rather than a person who deserves proportional intervention.
6. Integrity and route states are not yet transparent enough to explain when the system is degraded.
7. The audit trail exists but does not yet foreground access transparency for sensitive actions.

## 7 design corrections
1. Add child dignity mode and make it visible in both controls and incident handling.
2. Add guarded reveal with a stated reason and audit record.
3. Gate restrictive actions behind higher confidence or higher severity.
4. Reframe medium-confidence incidents as check-in suggestions, not lock-first moments.
5. Show a guardian transparency card that logs reveal, escalate, approve, and rule changes.
6. Show degraded coverage when device integrity weakens so the product does not overclaim certainty.
7. Keep privacy defaults visually stronger than intervention defaults.

## Recommended child dignity mode
Child dignity mode: low-confidence and routine-adjacent incidents stay in a review lane that suggests a calm parent check-in, suppresses remote restrictions by default, and avoids revealing raw content unless a guardian explicitly unlocks it.

## Recommended guardian audit transparency feature
Guardian audit transparency: a visible history of every acknowledgement, reveal request, escalation, approval, block, rule change, and resolution outcome, including whether the action exposed additional child data.
