# Role 8: Final Judge Re-Run

## Rescored categories
- Originality: 8/10
- Technical credibility: 7.5/10
- Demo strength: 8.5/10
- Product realism: 7.5/10
- Trust/safety design: 9/10
- Overuse of AI tropes: 8.5/10
- UI substance vs UI theater: 8/10
- Memorability: 8/10

## What improved
- The product now has a visible incident pipeline rather than just alert cards.
- Guardian actions change incident stage, route state, follow-up outcome, and the transparency trail.
- The privacy claim is now operational because guarded reveal is a real interaction with an audit footprint.
- Confidence and dignity mode now influence what a guardian can do, which makes the safety framing feel more principled.
- Device integrity now affects coverage language and escalation posture instead of living as decorative status text.

## What still remains weak
- The system is still simulated rather than integrated into a real device OS or messaging stack.
- Coverage degradation is believable, but still demo-driven rather than backed by real telemetry.
- The classifier is still rule-based, so a deep technical judge may ask how this generalizes beyond scripted examples.
- The product would still benefit from one more live state bridge between Controls and Alerts, such as a policy change immediately altering an active incident recommendation.

## Overall read
This is no longer just a polished themed dashboard. It is now a genuinely more convincing family safety product demo with a visible privacy model, an operational incident pipeline, and stronger trust design. It still lives in the category of believable hackathon prototype rather than production-ready system, but it is much harder to dismiss as a generic AI wrapper.

## Single strongest thing to mention in judging
SignalSafe does not default to full surveillance: it keeps most analysis on-device, sends a narrow redacted packet first, and logs every deeper reveal or guardian intervention in a family transparency trail.
