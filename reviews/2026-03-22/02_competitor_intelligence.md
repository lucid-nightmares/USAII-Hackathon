# Role 2: Competitor Intelligence Lead

Using the judge findings as the baseline pressure.

## What competitors do better today
- They feel operational. Events enter a pipeline, move through queues, escalate, and close with visible handling states.
- They treat policies as active controls, not just descriptive settings.
- They connect device integrity and coverage health to trust in the system.
- They make adult workflows concrete: triage, approve, block, escalate, hand off, and document.
- They expose enough action history that an operator can reconstruct what happened.

## What we should adopt conceptually
- A visible event pipeline rather than isolated alert cards.
- Escalation timers and route state that react to acknowledgement and confidence.
- Coverage health or tamper signals tied to monitoring trust.
- Approval and policy changes that immediately affect system state.
- Auditability for sensitive actions such as reveals and escalations.

## What we should NOT copy
- Full transcript feeds.
- Always-on surveillance framing.
- School or enterprise language around student management.
- Aggressive policing defaults.
- High-volume alert spam as proof of capability.

## Privacy-first reinterpretations
- Replace full content review with redacted packet first, guarded reveal only when justified.
- Replace broad historical tracking with routine context and short-lived incident views.
- Replace punitive control-first actions with check-in-first actions for uncertain cases.
- Replace admin audit with guardian transparency: who saw what, when, and why.
- Replace always-on coverage claims with explicit integrity state and degraded-mode communication.

## Top 5 behavior upgrades to implement
1. Incident state machine with route state and timestamps.
2. Guarded reveal flow with audit entry and visible trust boundary.
3. Confidence-aware escalation rules that downgrade uncertain incidents into dignity-first recommendations.
4. Device integrity state that can fall into degraded coverage and affect guardian trust.
5. A single action history that records acknowledgements, reveals, approvals, route changes, and resolution outcomes.
